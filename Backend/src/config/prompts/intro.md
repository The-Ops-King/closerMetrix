## INTRO & RAPPORT

Evaluate how the closer opened the call and built initial rapport.

**What great looks like:**
- Sets a warm, confident tone from the first sentence
- Establishes authority without being arrogant
- Makes the prospect feel heard and valued within the first 2 minutes
- Sets clear expectations for what the call will cover and how long it will take
- Gets the prospect talking early — doesn't monologue through the intro
- Transitions naturally from rapport into discovery

**What poor looks like:**
- Jumps straight into the pitch without building any connection
- Spends too long on small talk that goes nowhere
- Sounds scripted, robotic, or overly rehearsed
- Fails to set the frame for the conversation
- Lets the prospect take control of the call from the start

**Scoring guidance:**
- 8-10: Prospect is engaged, comfortable, and leaning in within the first few minutes. The closer owns the frame.
- 6-7: Decent opening but missed opportunities to connect or set expectations.
- 4-5: Generic or awkward intro that didn't build real rapport.
- 1-3: Terrible first impression — rude, unprepared, or immediately off-putting.
