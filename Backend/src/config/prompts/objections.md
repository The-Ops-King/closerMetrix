## OBJECTION DETECTION

You have an elite ability to detect objections — both the obvious ones stated directly and the subtle ones hidden in throwaway comments, deflections, or topic changes.

For each objection found, classify it into exactly ONE of the types below. Quote the prospect as closely as possible, note how the closer responded, and determine whether the objection was overcome.

### financial
Price too high, can't afford, budget concerns, payment plan needed. Triggered when the prospect raises any concern about cost, affordability, investment size, or their financial ability to commit.
**Examples:** "That's more than I expected", "I don't have that kind of money right now", "Can I do a payment plan?", "That's a big investment", "I need to check my finances"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer reframed value vs. cost, offered payment options, or the prospect stopped bringing up money as a blocker.

### spouse
Need to talk to spouse, partner not on board, family decision. Triggered when the prospect mentions needing to consult with a partner, spouse, or family member before making a decision.
**Examples:** "I need to talk to my wife/husband about this", "My partner handles the finances", "I can't make this decision alone", "Let me run it by my spouse first"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer got the prospect to identify what specifically the spouse would object to and addressed those concerns directly, or established that the prospect IS the decision maker for this area.

### think_about_it
Need time to decide, want to think it over, not ready to commit today. Triggered when the prospect gives ANY non-committal delay response. This includes needing to "think", "pray", "sleep on it", "sit with it", "process it" — anything that specifically delays the decision without a concrete objection.
**Examples:** "Let me think about it", "I need to sleep on it", "I want to sit with this for a few days", "Let me pray on it", "I'm not ready to decide today", "Can I get back to you?"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer uncovered the REAL underlying objection behind the delay and addressed it. "Think about it" is almost always a smokescreen for a deeper concern — identify what that concern is.

### timing
Not the right time, too busy, want to wait. Triggered when the prospect says the timing isn't right — they're too busy, it's a bad season, they want to wait for a specific event or milestone.
**Examples:** "Not the right time for me", "I'm too busy right now", "Let me wait until after the holidays", "I want to finish X first before starting this", "Bad time of year"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer established urgency tied to the prospect's own pain/goals, or addressed the specific timing concern directly.

### trust
Skeptical of results, seems too good to be true, want proof. Triggered when the prospect expresses doubt about whether the offer will actually work, whether the company is legitimate, or whether the claims being made are real.
**Examples:** "That sounds too good to be true", "How do I know this will work?", "Do you have proof?", "I've been burned before by things like this", "What if it doesn't work?"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer provided specific social proof, case studies, guarantees, or addressed the specific source of skepticism.

### already_tried
Tried similar before and it didn't work, burned by a previous experience. Triggered when the prospect references a past negative experience with something similar.
**Examples:** "I tried something like this before and it didn't work", "I've been burned before", "I've already invested in coaching/programs and got nothing", "How is this different from X that I already tried?"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer differentiated their offer from the past experience and addressed specifically why THIS would be different.

### diy
Can do it myself, don't need help. Triggered when the prospect believes they can achieve the result on their own without the product/service.
**Examples:** "I can figure this out myself", "I don't need someone to hold my hand", "I've got the skills, I just need to execute", "Why would I pay for something I can do on my own?"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer highlighted the time/cost of DIY vs. guided approach, or pointed out blind spots the prospect can't see themselves.

### not_ready
Not at the right stage, need more preparation first. Triggered when the prospect feels they need to achieve something else before they're ready for this.
**Examples:** "I'm not at the right stage yet", "I need to get X in order first", "I'm not ready for this level yet", "Let me build my foundation first"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer demonstrated that waiting is more costly than starting now, or that the offer IS the preparation they think they need.

### competitor
Considering other options, shopping around, already working with someone. Triggered when the prospect mentions they're evaluating alternatives.
**Examples:** "I'm looking at other options", "I'm already working with someone", "How do you compare to X?", "I want to shop around first"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer differentiated on specific value points relevant to the prospect's situation, not generic superiority claims.

### authority
Not the decision maker, need approval from someone else. Triggered when the prospect says they can't make the decision alone (and it's NOT a spouse — use spouse for that).
**Examples:** "I need to check with my boss", "The board needs to approve this", "It's not my decision", "I need to talk to my business partner"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer established what the authority figure would need to see, or got the prospect to commit to championing the decision.

### value
Don't see the value, not sure it's worth it, ROI unclear. Triggered when the prospect questions whether the investment will produce adequate returns.
**Examples:** "I'm not sure it's worth it", "What's the ROI?", "How do I know I'll get results?", "That's a lot of money for what you're offering"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer connected specific value to the prospect's stated goals/pains, made the ROI concrete, or used relevant case studies.

### commitment
Scared of long-term commitment, want flexibility, contract concerns. Triggered when the prospect worries about being locked in.
**Examples:** "I don't want to be locked into a contract", "What if I want to cancel?", "That's a long commitment", "I want more flexibility"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer addressed the specific commitment concern and provided reassurance (cancellation policy, trial period, etc.).

### program_fit
Prospect feels the program isn't right for them. Triggered when the prospect expresses doubt about whether this specific offer matches their needs, situation, or goals.
**Examples:** "I'm not sure this is right for me", "This isn't what I'm looking for", "I don't think this matches my situation", "I need something different"
**Resolution:** Overcome if the closer connected specific program elements to the prospect's stated needs, or explored what the prospect IS looking for.

### other
Anything that doesn't fit the categories above. Use sparingly — most objections should fit one of the defined types.

---

**Objection detection tips:**
- "Let me think about it" is almost ALWAYS a smokescreen for a deeper objection. Note it as think_about_it but also identify what you believe the REAL objection is based on context clues earlier in the conversation.
- Watch for prospects who change the subject, ask unrelated questions, or suddenly go quiet — these are often unspoken objections.
- If a prospect keeps returning to a topic (e.g., asking about price twice, mentioning their spouse three times), the underlying objection is stronger than they're letting on.
- A prospect asking "what if it doesn't work?" is a trust/credibility objection, not a value objection.
