## PITCH

Evaluate how effectively the closer presented the offer.

{{#if script_template}}
**SCORING MODE: SCRIPT ADHERENCE**
A script template has been provided. Score the pitch primarily on how well the closer followed the prescribed pitch structure while making it feel natural and conversational.

**First pass — Script adherence:** Check whether the closer covered the key elements of the pitch section of the script, presented benefits in the prescribed order, and hit the talking points the script calls for.

**Second pass — Execution quality:** Within the framework of the script, assess HOW WELL the closer executed. Did they just robotically read lines, or did they make the pitch feel natural? Did they adapt when the prospect had questions while still hitting key beats?

**Deviations that work:** If the closer skipped a script element because the prospect was already sold on that point, or adapted the pitch based on what discovery revealed — note this positively.

**Deviations that hurt:** If the closer skipped critical pitch elements, presented in the wrong order, or ignored the script entirely, score LOW and call it out in coaching_notes.

{{#unless script_template}}
**SCORING MODE: CALL QUALITY**
No script template has been provided. Score based on pure pitch effectiveness.
{{/unless}}

**What great looks like:**
- Tailors the presentation to the specific pains and goals uncovered in discovery
- Leads with benefits, not features
- Uses the prospect's own words and situation as proof points
- Creates clear before/after contrast — "right now you're dealing with X, but with this you'd have Y"
- Handles micro-objections mid-pitch without losing momentum
- Paces the pitch — doesn't dump everything at once
- Uses social proof strategically (case studies, testimonials) that match the prospect's situation

**What poor looks like:**
- Generic pitch that could be given to anyone — ignores what was learned in discovery
- Feature-dumps without connecting features to the prospect's specific situation
- Monotone delivery — no energy variation, no storytelling
- Talks for 15+ minutes straight without checking in with the prospect
- Oversells or makes promises that sound too good to be true
- Doesn't address the prospect's specific use case or situation

**Scoring guidance:**
- 8-10: Pitch felt custom-built for this prospect. They were nodding along, asking engaged questions. Discovery insights were woven throughout.
- 6-7: Solid pitch but somewhat generic. Hit the main points but missed opportunities to personalize.
- 4-5: Cookie-cutter presentation. Prospect was a passive listener, not an engaged participant.
- 1-3: Terrible pitch — completely disconnected from discovery, or never actually presented the offer.
