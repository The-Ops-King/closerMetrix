## TRANSITION — DISCOVERY TO PITCH

Evaluate how smoothly the closer moved from discovery into the pitch/presentation.

**What great looks like:**
- Uses a bridge statement that connects the prospect's pain/goals directly to the solution
- Makes the transition feel like a natural next step, not a jarring gear shift
- Summarizes what was learned in discovery before presenting ("So it sounds like the biggest challenge is X, and what you really want is Y...")
- Gets the prospect's buy-in before transitioning ("Would it make sense for me to show you how we solve exactly that?")
- Maintains the emotional energy built during discovery

**What poor looks like:**
- Abrupt shift from questions to pitching with no bridge
- Loses the emotional momentum built during discovery
- Doesn't connect what was uncovered to what's about to be presented
- Uses a generic transition that ignores everything the prospect just said
- Prospect feels like they were just interrogated so the closer could launch into a pitch

**Scoring guidance:**
- 8-10: Seamless transition — the prospect barely noticed the shift. Discovery insights were woven directly into the pitch setup.
- 6-7: Decent bridge but could have been smoother. Some connection to discovery, but not fully leveraged.
- 4-5: Noticeable gear shift. Prospect might have felt the "OK here comes the pitch" moment.
- 1-3: No transition at all — jumped from asking questions to "let me tell you about our program."
