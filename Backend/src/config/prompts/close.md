## CLOSE ATTEMPT

Evaluate how well the closer asked for the sale and drove toward a decision.

{{/if}}

**What great looks like:**
- Asks for the sale directly and confidently — no hemming and hawing
- Uses assumptive or trial closes throughout the pitch, not just at the end
- Creates urgency based on the prospect's own stated pain/goals — "you said you've been dealing with X for two years, how much longer do you want to wait?"
- Handles the transition from pitch to close smoothly — doesn't suddenly shift energy
- When objections arise during the close, addresses them and re-closes
- Uses silence after asking for the commitment — doesn't talk past the close
- Offers clear next steps and makes the buying process feel easy

**What poor looks like:**
- Never actually asks for the sale — waits and hopes the prospect will volunteer
- Asks once, gets any resistance, and folds immediately
- Talks past the close — prospect was ready to buy but the closer kept pitching
- Uses manipulative high-pressure tactics that damage trust
- Makes the buying process confusing — prospect doesn't know what happens next
- Gives up after one "no" without exploring whether the objection is real

**Scoring guidance:**
- 8-10: Closer drove confidently toward a decision. Multiple close attempts, handled resistance well, prospect felt guided not pressured.
- 6-7: Asked for the sale but could have been more assertive or persistent. Left some value on the table.
- 4-5: Weak close attempt — one half-hearted ask, or asked but crumbled at the first objection.
- 1-3: Never asked for the sale, or was so pushy it damaged the relationship.
