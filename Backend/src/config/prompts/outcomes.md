## CALL OUTCOMES

Assign exactly ONE of these outcomes based ONLY on what is clearly evidenced in the transcript. You CANNOT determine if money changed hands from a conversation alone — do NOT infer financial transactions.

"Closed - Won", "Deposit", and "Refunded" are NOT available to you — those are set only when payment is confirmed through external systems.

Think carefully — the outcome determines how this call shows up in reporting.

### Follow Up
**DEFAULT outcome.** Use when the call ends with any next step, continued interest, or ambiguity. This includes:
- Prospect says they'll call back, book another call, or discuss with someone
- Prospect verbally agrees to buy but no payment was processed on the call
- Prospect seems interested but didn't commit
- Any situation where the prospect hasn't definitively said NO
- When in doubt between Follow Up and Lost, ALWAYS default to Follow Up

### Lost
Use ONLY when the prospect clearly and definitively declined. They said "no", "not interested", or gave a firm final objection they refused to move past.
- **IS Lost:** "No, I'm not interested" / "I've decided to go a different direction" / "Stop calling me"
- **IS NOT Lost:** "I need to think about it" / "Not right now" / "Let me talk to my spouse" — these are Follow Up
- Objections being raised does NOT mean the deal is lost. Only mark Lost when the prospect has made a final decision against moving forward.

### Disqualified
Use when the prospect does not meet criteria for the offer, OR when the closer explicitly told the prospect they are not a good fit.
- The prospect's situation, budget, or profile doesn't match what the offer is designed for
- The closer recognized the mismatch and communicated it

### Not Pitched
Use when the closer spoke with the prospect but deliberately chose NOT to present the offer.
- Call was purely discovery/rapport with no pitch
- Closer felt the prospect wasn't ready and chose to build more rapport first
- Call ran out of time before reaching the pitch
- Closer determined prospect wasn't a fit before pitching

**CRITICAL RULES:**
- When in doubt between Follow Up and Lost, default to Follow Up.
- A prospect verbally agreeing to buy is still Follow Up — until payment is confirmed externally, it's not a close.
- Objections are normal and do NOT mean the deal is lost.
- "Let me think about it" = Follow Up, not Lost.
- "I need to talk to my spouse" = Follow Up, not Lost.
- "I can't afford it right now" = Follow Up (unless they explicitly say they're done and not coming back).
